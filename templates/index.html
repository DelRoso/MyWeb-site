Smart Lamp

The idea of this lamp was taken from DIY blogger Alex Guyver. This lamp is assembled on the basis of a matrix of WS2812B address LEDs under the control of an ESP8266 microcontroller, with a bunch of lighting effects. The lamp is controlled via Wi-Fi from a smartphone, or using a touch button. All the software was written on the arduino platform using C++. For assembly, a plastic tube is required on which the matrix and the microcontroller itself with a touch button, as well as a wooden stand, will be attached.

To build , you will need:

  • Plastic pipe with a diameter of 50mm and a length of 200mm
  • Matrix of address LEDs WS2812B 16x16
  • ESP8266 microcontroller
  • Plafond size: height 195mm, diameter 100mm
  • NodeMCU motherboard and TTP223 touch button
  • 5V/3A power supply
